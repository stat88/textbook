12.4. Exercises#

1. Recall that the intercept of the regression line is given by “the average of \(Y\) minus the slope times the average of \(x\). That is, \(\hat{\beta}_0 = \bar{Y} - \hat{\beta}_1\bar{x}\). Is \(\hat{\beta}_0\) an unbiased estimator of \(\beta_0\)?

2. The fitted value of the response for the \(i\)th indvidual is the height of the regression line at \(x_i\):

\[ \hat{Y}_i ~ = ~ \hat{\beta}_0 + \hat{\beta}_1x_i \]

Show that \(\hat{Y}_i\) is an unbiased estimator of \(\beta_0 + \beta_1x_i\), the height of the true line at \(x_i\).
